# Campaign Constitution

## Summary

The Campaign Constitution is the top design authority for Platinum & Bramble — the short, load-bearing statement of what the campaign is *about* and how it should be built. It sets the core premise (the meeting of North and Eastern Forest, neither wholly right), the core themes, the design principles that govern what gets authored, and the campaign's current center of gravity. Where the other bible notes elaborate, the Constitution governs: [[Tone & Themes]] and [[Overview]] expand on it and defer to it. New canon should be checked against the principles here.

## Core Premise

Platinum & Bramble is a campaign about the meeting point between the North and the Eastern Forest.

The North carries oaths, steel, memory, ruin, duty, and broken orders.

The Eastern Forest carries old powers, living wilderness, hidden law, spirits, thorns, and ancient memory.

Neither side is wholly right.

Neither side is wholly wrong.

The story lives in the tension between them.

## Core Themes

- Oaths
- Memory
- Duty
- Corruption
- Belonging
- Sacrifice
- Old powers awakening
- Civilization versus wilderness
- The cost of keeping peace
- The danger of forgotten things returning

## Design Principles

Mystery is more valuable than explanation.

Folklore is more valuable than history.

Adventure is more valuable than lore.

Relationships are more valuable than isolated facts.

Consequences are more valuable than static worldbuilding.

Every major note should create future stories.
