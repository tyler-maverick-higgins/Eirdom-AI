# Overview

> **Defers to:** [[Campaign Constitution]] — the top design authority. This note expands on the Constitution's premise and campaign gravity; where they touch, the Constitution governs.

## Summary
*Platinum & Bramble* opens at the edge of the wild, where the ordered North gives way to the old Eastern forest — at a crossing-place where the two worlds have always met and traded and distrusted one another. Two unlikely companions begin here: Eirik Vandr, a northern oath-knight keeping faith with an order that no longer exists, and Pip, an ancient of the Eastern wood who has woken from long sleep because something is wrong. Their story begins not with a quest handed to them, but with an inciting event at the crossing — a wrongness that pulls them both in, for their own reasons. Beneath it, slowly, a power is rising in the deep forest. What it is, the campaign does not yet say.

## The Setting at the Start
We begin at **a place of crossing on the edge of the wild** — the seam made concrete. Picture it: the last settled holding before the trees close in, built where an old road crosses from North into East. A bridge, or a ford, or a mountain pass; a place where northern traders and eastern wood-folk meet under an uneasy truce older than anyone living. To the west and behind: the ordered world of halls, oaths, and hard winters. To the east and ahead: the deep forest that remembers, where the folktales are true and the old things sleep. The crossing belongs fully to neither. That is exactly why the story starts here.

> [!note] Invented — react freely
> This crossing-place needs a name and a face, and that is yours to give (it will become a [[Locations/]] note). I have left it as a *type* of place rather than a fixed one so you and Irina can shape it. Is it a bridge-town? A ford with an inn? A pass with a waystation? The answer sets the campaign's first stage.

## The Two Who Begin Here
*(This is where the framework is yours and the connective tissue is invented. Treat the "how they met" as a first draft.)*

**Eirik Vandr** — a knight of a broken order, wandering the seam, keeping an oath no one is left to witness. He is in the North-facing world but no longer fully of it; the order that gave him purpose is gone. He has drifted to the edge of the wild because a man with nothing left to protect goes looking for something that still needs protecting.

**Pip** — an ancient of the Eastern wood, lately woken, who sensed a corruption she recognized and did not like. She has come to the seam from the forest side because whatever is wrong is wrong *here*, at the edge, where her wood meets the world of men.

**How they meet (invented — your call to keep or replace):** They arrive at the same wrongness from opposite directions — the knight from the ordered side, the ancient from the wild side — and find that neither can resolve it alone. He has a sword and an oath and no knowledge of the old things; she has four centuries of knowledge and the body of a creature the size of a child's knee. Each is what the other lacks. That is the seam made into a partnership: one of the North, one of the East, meeting where the worlds meet.

## How It Begins — The Inciting Event
You chose a **loud start**: the campaign opens on something already gone wrong. Here is a first draft of what that could be — not the answer, a shape to push against:

*Something has come through the crossing that should not have.* A traveler arrives wrong — changed, or carrying something, or dead in a way that isn't natural. Or the forest's edge has moved overnight, closer than it was, and a holding that stood yesterday is gone. Or the old truce that keeps the crossing peaceful has broken, and northern and eastern folk are at each other's throats over something neither understands. Whatever it is, it is the kind of wrongness that a knight cannot fix with a sword alone and an ancient cannot fix without hands that can hold one — so the two are thrown together by it on the first night.

> [!note] The inciting event is the most important thing to get right, and it is yours
> I have given three flavors above (a wrong traveler / the moving forest / the broken truce). They are starting points. The right one depends on what you and Irina want the campaign to *feel* like in its first hour. We should choose and sharpen this together before the first session — it is the door everything else opens through.

## The Thread Beneath — A Power Rising
You asked for **a power rising in the forest, left open** — and that is exactly how it should be held. Something old is stirring in the deep East. The inciting event at the crossing is its first ripple, not its center. Neither Eirik nor Pip understands it yet; Pip *recognizes* the shape of it (the corruption she woke for) but does not yet know its name or its full scale.

This is deliberately undefined. In keeping with the worldbuilding discipline, the rising power should **emerge through play** rather than being fixed now — so it can grow to fit the campaign you actually run, and so its slow revelation carries weight. What is known: it is old, it is in the forest, it is connected to whatever woke Pip, and it is getting stronger. What it *is* — a returning thing, a spreading corruption, a power that was sealed and is no longer — is left for the table to discover.

> [!warning] Leave this open on purpose
> The temptation is to decide right now what the rising power is. Resist it. A threat revealed slowly, that grows to fit the story you tell, lands harder than one fully specified before session one. Let the campaign earn its answer.

## The Scope
This is built to be a **large campaign** — a long story, not a one-shot. The structure supports that: a personal, local beginning at the crossing that widens outward as the rising power reveals itself, the two protagonists growing from "two strangers thrown together by one bad night" toward whatever they become. The scale starts intimate (a place, a wrongness, two people) and grows. That is the right shape for a long game: begin small enough to care, leave room to become epic.

## What We Know vs. What Is Open
**Established (canon):**
- We begin at a crossing-place on the edge of the wild, the seam between North and East.
- Eirik Vandr (northern oath-knight) and Pip (ancient of the Eastern wood) are the two protagonists.
- The start is a loud one — an inciting event, something already wrong.
- A power is rising in the deep forest; it connects to whatever woke Pip.
- The campaign is long-form, beginning intimate and widening.

**Open (to decide, or to emerge from play):**
- The name and nature of the crossing-place.
- The specific inciting event.
- How exactly Eirik and Pip met.
- What the rising power actually is.
- What broke Eirik's order, and what it has to do with any of this.

> [!note] The honest state of this note
> The framework here is from the GM's choices. Most of the specifics are invented to give the table something concrete to react to, and are explicitly marked open above. This Overview is a launch point, not a finished history — it gives the campaign a place to stand and a direction to walk, and leaves the load-bearing mysteries for the table to answer.
