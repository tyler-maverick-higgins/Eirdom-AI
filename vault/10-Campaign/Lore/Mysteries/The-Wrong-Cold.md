# The Wrong Cold

## Summary

Something old is rising in the deep [[The Ashwood|Ashwood]], and the first sign of it is a *wrong cold* — a frost that pours out of an afflicted person from the inside, opening a door through their body for the thing in the East to come through. The campaign opens on its first ripple: a stranger arrives at [[Whisperbridge]] rimed in this cold, slowly becoming a door, savable only by those who understand what is being done to them ([[First Session — The Traveler at the Crossing]]). [[Pip]] half-recognizes the cold's shape from centuries ago and does not like it. This is the central mystery of the campaign and the thread beneath everything — what the power is, what it wants, and what it will cost to stop. It is meant to be revealed slowly, earned through play, never resolved early.

## Known Facts

Information established as true and discoverable in play.

* The affliction presents first as a *wrong cold* — frost that spreads from inside the body outward, not from the weather inward. Where it spreads, flesh goes grey-white and cold-burned, and the person *recedes*.
* It is not frostbite and not contagious on touch; the afflicted can be approached, restrained, and carried.
* As the cold advances, the person speaks words that are not quite theirs, in a flat, far-away voice — and less of them answers over time.
* The affliction comes from the East; afflicted travelers come *out of* the Ashwood.
* It is **winnable** — the afflicted can be saved, but only by understanding what is happening to them, and the answer lies east toward the source.
* [[Pip]] recognizes the *quality* of the cold — a reaching, wrong cold, distinct from honest winter cold — from a very long time ago.

## Rumors

What people believe (mostly the fearful, partial knowledge of the Seam and the North).

* It is just a hard season, a bad winter come early. *(False — see GM-only.)*
* The wood-folk have sent a curse, or the forest itself is angry.
* Travelers out of the deep East "come back wrong," and have for longer than this.
* The truce at the ford was sworn over something buried there that is stirring now.

## Competing Theories

### Theory One — A natural affliction

A sickness, a frost-plague, a thing of the body that medicine or warmth might cure. *Partly testable in play; the cold's behavior defies it.*

### Theory Two — A curse or working

Someone or something has *done* this — a witch, a spirit, a deliberate malice out of the wood. *Closer; misattributes the agency.*

### Theory Three — A door, not a disease

The body is not afflicted but *used* — a threshold being opened through, with the cold as what comes first through the gap. The afflicted person is becoming a way in for something else. *Of the three, the hardest to arrive at and the hardest to act on.*

## Evidence

Clues discovered so far.

* The rime-pattern spreading across an afflicted person's skin — a shape Pip has seen cold move in before, which is not natural.
* The word(s) the afflicted repeat from the other side of the cold — which Pip knows, from long ago.
* The direction every thread points: east, toward the source.

## GM-Only — The Truth Behind the Cold

**Status of this section:** the *constraints* below are fixed canon and the assistants should treat them as true (once ingestion is GM-aware). The *identity* of the power is deliberately left open even to the GM, to be earned in play. Build forward within these constraints; do not contradict them, and do not prematurely close what is marked open.

### What it IS (fixed constraints)

* **Theory Three is correct.** The door-not-disease reading is the truth the players must reach to save anyone ([[First Session — The Traveler at the Crossing]]). Theory One (natural affliction) is false; Theory Two (a sent curse / deliberate malice) misattributes the agency. The player-facing Competing Theories section is written neutrally on purpose — do not let an assistant or a read-aloud reveal which is true.
* **It is old** — older than the Holdlands, present in the Ashwood long before the North's law reached the border. Pip's recognition is real memory, not coincidence.
* **It was contained or diminished before, not destroyed.** Roughly four centuries ago something set it back; it did not end. This is why it is *rising* now rather than arriving for the first time, and why Pip has seen its shape before.
* **It works through thresholds.** The door-through-a-body is not a one-off trick — it is *how the power operates*. It opens ways through. The wrong cold is the leading edge of an opening, the way cold pours through a door cracked onto winter. Afflictions, and likely other phenomena, are thresholds being forced.

### What it is NOT (constraints that kill bad theories)

* **It is not a god**, and not a devil or demon in any cosmic/pantheon sense. Keep it grounded; this world's magic is rare and unsettling, not high-fantasy theology. (In-world Theory Two's "deliberate malice / sent curse" misattributes the agency.)
* **It is not natural cold or weather.** The "hard season" rumor is *false*. The wrongness is the entire point; the cold is a symptom of an opening, not a climate.
* **It is not yet fully through.** At campaign start it is *rising / reaching*, not arrived. The afflicted traveler is the *first* door, not the last. The failure state of the first session — the traveler becoming a door at Whisperbridge — would be a breach, not the arrival of the whole.

### Deliberately OPEN (undecided even for the GM — let play decide)

* The power's **name**.
* Its **will and intent** — whether it *wants* anything, or merely spreads the way cold spreads.
* Its **final scale** — local horror, regional threat, or something larger.
* **What set it back four centuries ago** — fully open; not tied to any authored cause yet. (Pip's memory is the road in, but the answer is unwritten.)

### Why this is walled off

Per the campaign's founding discipline: a threat revealed slowly, that grows to fit the story actually told, lands harder than one specified before play. The constraints exist so the assistants and the GM never contradict the spine; the open list exists so the heart of the mystery is genuinely earned, not pre-decided.
