# The Ford-Wardens

## Summary

The Ford-Wardens are the small, stretched body that keeps the truce at the crossings of [[The Seam]] — the closest thing to authority in a border country that belongs to neither the [[The Holdlands|North]] nor the [[The Ashwood|East]]. They are not a garrison and not a court; they are truce-keepers, drawn from both worlds, sworn to hold the fords neutral and open so North and East can meet without bloodshed. Their chief seat is [[Whisperbridge]], where the [[The Warden of Whisperbridge|Warden]] holds the peace and the innkeeper answers to them. The Wardens are old, undermanned, and run more on reputation and custom than on force — which is exactly why they matter and why they are fragile. When the wrong stranger arrives and the truce begins to strain, the Wardens are who the crossing looks to, and who may not be enough.

## Purpose

To keep the crossings of the Seam neutral and open. The Wardens exist so that the truce between North and East is *held by someone* rather than left to chance — so that a Northern trader and an eastern wood-dweller can share a hearth at the ford without it ending in blood, and so the roads through the border country stay passable. They keep the peace, mediate disputes, and guard the crossings against those who would close or seize them. They take no side between the two worlds; their whole legitimacy rests on belonging to neither.

## Goals

* Hold the truce at Whisperbridge and the lesser crossings, especially as fear out of the East tests it.
* Keep the fords open and travelers moving despite the rising strangeness.
* Avoid being forced to take a side between North and East — the moment they do, they stop being Wardens.
* (Quietly, and not all of them know it — see *Secrets*.)

## Leadership

* [[The Warden of Whisperbridge]] *(proposed)* — the Warden seated at the chief crossing, de-facto head of the Wardens and the figure who actually holds the peace at the ford. The innkeeper of Whisperbridge answers to them. The first real authority the players reckon with when the wrong stranger arrives. *Needs: a name, a face, which world they lean toward (if either).*

## Structure & Membership

A loose, thin, half-formal body — defined enough to have a name, a seat, and a head, but stretched too few across too much country to be a strong institution. Membership is drawn from **both worlds**: Northerners and eastern folk both wear the Warden's charge, because a truce that binds both sides can only be kept by both sides. One is named a Warden by the standing Wardens, not by any crown or hold; the charge is sworn, not inherited. In practice there are only a handful — a Warden at the chief ford, a few more spread across the lesser crossings, and local folk like the innkeeper who serve the truce without formally bearing the charge. They have no uniform beyond a known token or word; their authority is recognition, not insignia.

### Enemies

* [[Factions/]] *(open — the rising wrongness in the East is not a faction, but it is the Wardens' deepest threat; see [[The Wrong Cold]])*

## Assets & Reach

Seam-wide in charge but thin in fact. The Wardens hold the moral and customary authority to keep any crossing of the Seam, and that authority is genuinely respected by both worlds — their real asset. Materially they have little: no standing force, no treasury, no walls. Their reach is the network of crossings and the goodwill of those who use them. Stretch them across one real crisis and the thinness shows.

## Holdings

* [[Whisperbridge]] — the chief crossing and the Wardens' seat.
* The lesser crossings of [[The Seam]] *(unbuilt — exist in principle; built as play reaches them)*

**The truce was sworn over something buried at the ford — and it connects to the wrong cold.**

The Wardens are older than they look, and the truce they keep was not first sworn merely to let traders pass. At its origin it was sworn over *something at the ford* (the rumor on the Seam is a degraded memory of this, true), and that something connects to the power now rising in the East ([[The Wrong Cold]]).

**Fixed (constraints the campaign must honor):**

* The truce has a hidden original purpose beyond trade-peace, tied to the ford itself.
* That purpose connects to the wrong cold / the rising power — the Wardens are, without most of them knowing it, part of why it was *contained or diminished before* (see The Wrong Cold's GM-only constraint: it was set back ~4 centuries ago, not destroyed).
* Most living Wardens do **not** know this. The knowledge is lost, partial, or held by very few — possibly none.

**Deliberately OPEN (undecided even for the GM — let play decide):**

* *What* is buried at the ford, if anything physical is.
* *How* the truce-oath bears on the wrong cold — ward, seal, binding, memory, or something else.
* Whether any Warden alive still knows, or whether it survives only as the rumor and the habit of keeping the ford.
* Whether this ties to *what set the power back four centuries ago* — this is The Wrong Cold's open tier-3 question and must not be closed here. Plant the connection; do not resolve it.

**Why walled off:** founding discipline — plant the hook, earn the answer in play. This secret gives the Wardens a reason to matter to the central mystery without pre-deciding the mystery. Do not let founding a faction quietly resolve The Wrong Cold.

## Adventure Hooks

* **When the truce strains** — as fear of the wrong cold spreads, the Wardens struggle to keep North and East from turning on each other at the ford; the players may be asked to help, or to take the charge themselves.
* **Too few Wardens** — the body is stretched thin; a crossing left unkept, a Warden gone missing, a charge that needs bearing.
* **The Warden's regard** — the Warden of Whisperbridge is the authority the players must work with (or around) in the opening; earning or losing their trust shapes what the crossing will do.
* **Eirik and the charge** — an oath-knight of a broken order and a body of truce-sworn keepers are a natural fit; whether Eirik is drawn toward the Wardens is a live thread ([[Eirik Vandr]]).
