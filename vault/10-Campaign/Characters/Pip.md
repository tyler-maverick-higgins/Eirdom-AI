# Pip

> **Pronouns:** she/her · **Ancestry:** One of the old things of the Eastern forest
> **Home:** the hollow oak in the Eastern wood (see *Goals & Bonds*)
> **System note:** Cairn has no classes — see *How She Plays* below.

## Summary

Pip is one of the old things of the Eastern wood — a small, ancient forest-being, older than the northern kingdoms, who sleeps for years at a time inside a hollow oak and wakes when something is wrong. She is roughly the height of a child's knee, given to precise and archaic speech, and finds the world endlessly remarkable after four centuries of watching it. Her magic is the wood's own: nature at the roots, older will beneath it, the kind of power that produces the right direction if not always the intended result. She is beautiful and a little frightening, in the way the forest's oldest things always are.

## Background

*(Carried over from her origin — the soul of the character. High confidence; this is established and barely needed bending.)*
Pip is, by her own account, over four hundred years old, and this is difficult to verify. She has spent long stretches of those centuries in voluntary dormancy inside a hollowed oak she refers to, drily, as a place of her own. She woke most recently because she sensed a corruption she recognized and did not like. Her magic does not fit any tidy school — it is intuitive rather than learned, layered like a tree: nature at the roots, elemental force in the branches, and at its heart the old will-magic, the making of things happen through sheer conviction that they should. The results are not always what she intends. The direction is almost always right. There is a difference, she will tell you, and it matters.

She speaks in complete, precise sentences, asks questions constantly because she genuinely wants to know, and delivers alarming news in the same tone as pleasant news. She does not perform modesty or arrogance. She states what she knows, what she does not, and what she suspects.

## Arc

Who she is becoming: an ancient who has chosen, after centuries of sleeping through the world's troubles, to stay awake for this one. Why this corruption, why now, and what it costs a creature like her to care — that is hers to discover in play. **Revisit this as the campaign grows.**

## Voice

Precise, archaic, unhurried. No filler words. Sample lines (carried from her chronicle, all in character):

- "I have seen this symbol before. That was a bad period. I slept through most of it, which I regret."
- "The tree is not dead. It is angry. There is a difference and it matters to both of us."
- "That was not what I intended. The outcome is acceptable. Carry on."
- "The ground tastes wrong here. I mean that literally. I licked it. Do not look at me like that."
