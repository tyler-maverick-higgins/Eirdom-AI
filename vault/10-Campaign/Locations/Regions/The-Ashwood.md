# The Ashwood

## Summary

The Ashwood is the old Eastern forest — the wild, enchanted world that lies past [[The Seam]] and the ford at [[Whisperbridge]], deep woods that remember, where the folktales turn out to be true and the oldest things sleep. "Ashwood" is what the North calls it; the wood-folk and the old things within it have their own, far older name, which the world of men has not learned. It is Pip's country ([[Pip]]), older than the northern kingdoms, beautiful and frightening in equal measure. The North keeps its back to it and does not look too closely. Somewhere in its depths, a power is rising — the thread beneath the whole campaign — and the affliction that opens the story is its first ripple reaching the edge. The Ashwood is the direction the story walks: the answer lies east, toward the source. **It is deliberately unbuilt.** What it is, and what stirs in its heart, is left for play to earn.

> [!warning] Build this region last and slowly.
> The Ashwood is the campaign's destination and central mystery. Per the founding discipline, it is kept thin on purpose — naming the surface, never the depths. Do not commit what the rising power is, what the wood's true name is, or what lies at its heart. Let the table discover the East by walking into it.

## Geography

Old forest, deepening eastward from the treeline at the Seam. The near edge — what folk at Whisperbridge can see and occasionally enter — is forest like any cold-country forest, only older and quieter than it should be. Beyond that, almost nothing is established, and that is intentional: the deep Ashwood is unmapped, and the map should fill in only as the players cross it. Folk say the edge moves; whether that is true is an open question of the Seam.

## History

Established only in outline: the Ashwood is older than the Holdlands, older than the northern kingdoms — a world that was strange and enchanted long before the North's law reached the border. Its history is the wood-folk's, not the North's, and the world of men knows almost none of it. Everything specific is left open to emerge in play.
