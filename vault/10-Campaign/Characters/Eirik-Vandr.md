# Eirik Vandr

> **Pronouns:** he/him · **Ancestry:** Northern (human, of the Germanic halls)
> **System note:** Cairn has no classes — see *How He Plays* below.

## Summary

Eirik Vandr is a sworn oath-knight of the North — a man who kept a vow when everyone else who swore it had died, scattered, or forgotten. He is calm, deliberate, and carries a quiet sorrow that never fully leaves him. He believes that strength is owed to those who cannot defend themselves, and that mercy is the harder and truer measure of a fighting man. A raven follows him that he does not fully explain. He is not a wielder of great magic; he is a mortal who endured, touched once by something that has not entirely let him go.

## Background

*(Carried over from his origin — the soul of the character. High confidence; this is established.)*
Eirik was born to humble ground in the North, a farmer's son before he was ever a knight. He lost his home young to cruelty he could not stop in time, and swore then that no such fire would burn unchecked while he drew breath. He found his way to an order of oath-sworn — a hall of warriors bound by vow to protect — and was forged there into something tempered by mercy rather than rage. In the order's defining trial he faced the darker version of himself, the one that whispered vengeance was cleaner than virtue, and he refused it. He lowered his blade. That refusal is the center of who he is.

*(Invented to fit the world — first draft, react freely.)*
The order is gone now. What broke it, and how Eirik alone kept faith when the others fell away, is the wound he carries and a thread the campaign can pull. He has wandered the seam between North and East since, a knight of an order that no longer exists, keeping a vow no one is left to witness.

## Arc

Who he is becoming: a man learning whether an oath still means anything when there's no one left to hold him to it but himself. His sorrow is real but it has not hardened into bitterness — the question of the campaign is whether it stays that way. **Revisit this as the campaign grows.**

## The Raven

*(Reforged from his lost guardian — kept as presence, not a creature with a stat-block, per design.)*
A raven follows Eirik. He had something once — a guardian, a presence, a vow given form — and lost it, and the raven is what remains, or what returned, or what watches in its place. He does not explain it and may not fully understand it himself. It is the faint numinous edge in an otherwise grounded man: rare, unsettling, never routine. When the dark things draw near, the raven is uneasy first.

> [!note] Design note (delete once settled)
> This is the carryover of the celestial companion from his origin, reforged for a low-magic world. It is deliberately ambiguous — keep it a presence and a mystery rather than defining it mechanically. What it *is* can be answered through play, or left unanswered.
