# The Holdlands

## Summary

The Holdlands are the settled, ordered country of the North — the iron-and-rune world of longhouses and mead-halls, oaths sworn and kept, hard winters endured, and gods who demand more than they give. It is the world Eirik Vandr comes from, and the world that lies behind [[Whisperbridge]] and [[The Seam]] to the west. The name means *the land that holds*: ground kept against the cold and the dark by law, kinship, and the keeping of one's word. The North values honor, hospitality, and faith kept even when no one is left to witness it. The Holdlands are not the campaign's frontier — they are the known, grounded baseline the players come *from*, the warm hall at their backs as they walk east into the wild. Their cold is honest cold, which matters: it is the contrast against which the *wrong* cold of the Eastern affliction reads as violation.

## Geography

Settled northern country: cleared land, longhouse holdings, halls and the steadings around them, worked fields giving way to high cold moor and mountain. Hard winters are the defining condition — long, dark, and demanding, but *known* and prepared for. Rivers and old roads run down toward the Seam and the forest's edge. The Holdlands grow wilder and emptier the farther east one travels, until the holdings thin into the border country of the Seam and, beyond it, the Ashwood.

## History

What is established: the Holdlands are an old, settled order — a culture of halls, oaths, and law that has held its ground in a hard land for generations. Eirik's broken order of oath-knights arose from this world, though what it was, what broke it, and how Eirik alone kept faith are deliberately open threads ([[Eirik Vandr]]). The deeper history of the North is left to emerge in play.
